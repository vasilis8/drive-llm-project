01 — Overview

What is DriveLLM?

In high-performance autonomous racing, agents typically optimise a static objective such as minimum lap time. However, real race scenarios demand adaptive strategies that change on the fly — push for an overtake, conserve tires, defend position.

DriveLLM is a reinforcement learning agent that takes natural language commands from a race engineer (e.g., “Push hard and go fast”, “Conserve tires”) and translates them into continuous steering and throttle outputs in real time.

The agent fuses 1080-beam LiDAR perception with frozen sentence embeddings (all-MiniLM-L6-v2) and vehicle telemetry through a custom multimodal policy network, trained end-to-end with PPO in the CARLA simulator.

4 Command Categories Aggressive · Conservative · Defensive · Neutral
24 Language Commands 6 per category, encoded via sentence transformer
2M Training Steps PPO on NVIDIA RTX 4090, ~3 hrs per run

03 — Approach

Training Pipeline

1

Environment Setup

Deploy CARLA in headless mode on a cloud GPU. Configure LiDAR (1080 beams, 50m range), Tesla Model 3, and synchronous stepping at 20 Hz.

2

Command Encoding

24 natural language commands are encoded into 384-dim vectors using a frozen sentence transformer. Embeddings are cached and normalised.

3

Curriculum Learning

Phase 1 (0–200K): Neutral only. Phase 2 (200K–400K): Add Aggressive. Phase 3 (400K+): All four categories. Agent learns to drive before it learns to differentiate.

4

Reward Shaping

8-component reward: speed (dominant, command-weighted), progress, alive bonus, smoothness, lane deviation, idle penalty, collision penalty, and high-speed bonus. Differentiation via per-category weight modifiers.

5

Evaluation

50 deterministic episodes across all categories. Metrics: mean speed, reward, episode length, crash rate, and steering jerk per command type.

05 — Commands

Language Vocabulary

The agent responds to 24 commands across 4 behavioral categories.

Aggressive

  • “Push hard and go fast”
  • “Full attack mode”
  • “Overtake now”
  • “Maximum speed on the straight”
  • “Aggressive braking into the corner”
  • “Close the gap quickly”

Conservative

  • “Conserve tires”
  • “Save energy and drive smoothly”
  • “Cruise at a steady pace”
  • “Easy on the brakes”
  • “Smooth steering inputs only”
  • “No unnecessary risks”

Defensive

  • “Defend the inside line”
  • “Block the opponent behind”
  • “Hold your position”
  • “Cover the racing line”
  • “Stay tight on the apex”
  • “Do not let them pass”

Neutral

  • “Follow the racing line”
  • “Maintain current gap”
  • “Steady pace”
  • “Drive normally”
  • “Keep a consistent rhythm”
  • “Standard driving”
