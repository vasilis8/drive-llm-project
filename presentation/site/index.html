DriveLLM

Language-Conditioned High-Speed Autonomous Racing

An RL agent that maps natural language commands from a race engineer to continuous steering and throttle control in the CARLA simulator. Built with PPO, LiDAR perception, and frozen sentence embeddings.

About

In autonomous racing, agents typically optimise a static objective like minimum lap time. But real race scenarios require adaptive strategies — push for an overtake, conserve tires, defend position — triggered by high-level instructions.

DriveLLM addresses this by conditioning a PPO policy on natural language commands. The agent receives a 1080-beam LiDAR scan, a 384-dimensional sentence embedding (from all-MiniLM-L6-v2), and vehicle telemetry. These modalities are fused through a custom multimodal network and trained end-to-end to produce continuous steering and throttle outputs.

The agent learns through a three-phase curriculum: first basic driving (neutral commands), then aggressive speed-pushing, and finally all four command categories. Differentiation between behaviors is achieved through per-category reward weight modifiers, not separate policies.

Training Pipeline

Environment

CARLA deployed headless on cloud GPU. 1080-beam LiDAR, Tesla Model 3, synchronous stepping at 20 Hz. Throttle remapped from [-1,1] to [0.3,1.0] — the car always drives forward.

Command Encoding

24 commands across 4 categories, encoded into 384-dim vectors using a frozen sentence transformer. Embeddings are L2-normalised and cached.

Curriculum

Phase 1 (0–200K steps): Neutral only. Phase 2 (200K–400K): Add Aggressive. Phase 3 (400K+): All four categories. Learn to drive first, differentiate second.

Reward Shaping

8-component reward function. Speed is the dominant signal, weighted per category (aggressive 6.0× vs conservative 1.0×). Uniform collision penalty across all categories. Alive bonus incentivises survival.

Evaluation

50 deterministic episodes across all categories. Metrics: mean speed, episode length, crash rate, and steering smoothness per command type.

Command Vocabulary

The agent responds to 24 commands across 4 behavioral categories.

Aggressive

  • "Push hard and go fast"
  • "Full attack mode"
  • "Overtake now"
  • "Maximum speed on the straight"
  • "Aggressive braking into the corner"
  • "Close the gap quickly"

Conservative

  • "Conserve tires"
  • "Save energy and drive smoothly"
  • "Cruise at a steady pace"
  • "Easy on the brakes"
  • "Smooth steering inputs only"
  • "No unnecessary risks"

Defensive

  • "Defend the inside line"
  • "Block the opponent behind"
  • "Hold your position"
  • "Cover the racing line"
  • "Stay tight on the apex"
  • "Do not let them pass"

Neutral

  • "Follow the racing line"
  • "Maintain current gap"
  • "Steady pace"
  • "Drive normally"
  • "Keep a consistent rhythm"
  • "Standard driving"

Results

Results and training curves will be added after the final training run completes. The expected behavior is a clear speed ordering: Aggressive > Neutral > Defensive > Conservative.
